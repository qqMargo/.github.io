I am a final-year PhD candidate in Transportation Engineering at Chang’an University, with research experience at University College Dublin in Ireland. My work focuses on transport decarbonisation, life-cycle assessment of passenger vehicles, and data-driven analysis for carbon accounting and policy evaluation.

My current research combines life-cycle modelling, machine learning, scenario analysis, and regional assessment to better understand the environmental impacts of new energy vehicles and to support evidence-based transport policy.

About

My research is centred on sustainable transportation systems and the low-carbon transition of road transport. I am particularly interested in the life-cycle environmental impacts of passenger vehicles, multi-pollutant assessment, regional differences in energy systems, and the application of intelligent data-driven methods to transport carbon emission analysis.

Through my doctoral work, I have developed experience in combining transport engineering, environmental systems analysis, and quantitative modelling. I aim to continue building interdisciplinary research at the intersection of transportation, energy, environment, and public policy.
